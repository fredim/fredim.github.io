Save The Date Cards:

Not many people do save the date cards anymore, but we had an idea and thought it was really cute and decided to run with it. We found photos of each of us when we were little, turning them to black and white to keep the design clean and simple. Then we merged and printed the images to a card, and wrapped a vellum slip printed with the date and location of our wedding, and tied a green bow around it.

  
     

The Invitations:

Invites were hand made from scratch using green hard stock as the back paper, cut and folded into a pointed petal-shape design. Wedding details were printed against cream paper and pasted on top of a matching green decorative paper, topped with a small jewel at the top center. Additional information sheets and RSVP postcards were cut into sizes that when put together, cascaded down, revealing the title of each sheet. These sheets were then tied together with a green bow, placed in the middle of the invite with a square vellum piece above it. The petals of the invite were folded closed and sealed with a pretty cream ribbon. (Thanks Junko for your help!)

  
     

The Programs:

Thanks to Junko's help, these wonderful programs were successfully finished days before the wedding. We tried to tie in all the details of our invitations to our programs and table settings - using the same green decorative paper, ribbons, and jewels. The programs opened up into mini books, pages tied down with a green bow.

  
     

The Favors:

For our guests, we gave out beautiful scalloped-edge Asian parasols with a cherry blossom branch hand-painted on them. They originally were chosen purely for their beauty, but it ended up being a very very hot day with no cloud in sight. As it worked out, these parasols actually became very useful as everyone sought shelter from the hot sun.

 

  
     

The Table Setting:

The actual reception took place inside the Hill House, a long, wood-floored open room with a full length of windows that opened up to an amazing view of both the vinyard and the Bay Area below. Because the location was so beautiful and colorful on its own, we chose simple table decorations with peridot table cloth, cream napkins, chocolate name seating tags covered with matching green paper, and a beautiful orchid in a bamboo basket as the centerpiece.

  
     

The Bouquets:

Bouquets (and boutteniers) were handmade by Amy and her bridesmaids. The bride's bouquet was full of large cream roses, green orchids, freesia, verburnum and bear grass. Bridesmaids bouquets were comprised of large cream roses, jumbo white carnations, freesia and bear grass. It took lots of hard work and a coordinated effort to get these done the night before the wedding, but we did it. Thanks ladies for a job well done!

 

  
     

Doves:

Cheesy as it may sound, we had 24 doves released at our wedding. We decided that if there were ever to be doves released at a wedding, we were at the right location. The winery perched atop the Santa Cruz mountains made for the perfect setting. High in the mountains with a wide open view, and surrounded purely by nature's beauty...the dove release was actually quite spectacular. (We hope they made it home as there was a mischievious hawk flying overhead!)

  
     

The Cake:

We got our cake from Copenhagen's Bakery in Burlingame. We loved the cake when we first tried it at a birthday and chose to use them for our wedding. Trying to stay in line with the simplicity of our wedding, we chose pure cream colored frosting piped with a delicate design that matched our green decorative paper. Thanks to Christina and Claudia who helped decorate it with orchids.

  
     

The Band:

No DJ can compare to a live band, so we hired the Black Market Jazz Orchestra to play at our wedding. The five-piece band was great! You can hear them play Friday and Saturday nights at the Top of The Mark in San Francisco.
