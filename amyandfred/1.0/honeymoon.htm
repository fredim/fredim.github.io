Our Honeymoon
 

We finally decided on our honeymoon and are so excited for the trip. To start, we are going to take a 4-day Windjammer tour from Miami to the island of Nassau in the Bahamas. I'm not one for boats, but Fred loves them and upon looking at the details of the adventure, I had to admit, it looked pretty good. We'll be sailing on the beautiful S.V. Legacy.
 
 

This four-masted Barquentine, formerly the France II, was built in 1959 by Forges et al Meditterranee of Le Havre, France and served as a meteorological research vessel for the French Government. Acquired by Windjammer in 1989, she was renovated with custom interiors, spacious accommodations and state-of-the-art navigational systems. She blends nautical tradition with modern comfort.

Our trip begins in Miami, with stops at Birmini, Gun Cay, and finally Nassau.
 

Bimini is a legendary island known for big fish tournaments, wicked wreck diving, and spotted Atlantic dolphins that seek human interaction only in the wild. This mysterious site is said to be an ancient roadway leading to the sunken continent, Atlantis.

Gun Cay is a teensy-weensy islet floating in the bluest water on earth. The sunbathing, swimming, and snorkeling are all first class. Local population: zero.

Nassau/Paradise Island is the home of the Bahamian national capital, a bustling hub of The Bahamas since the shipwrecking days of the legendary pirate Blackbeard. Prized for its sheltered harbour, the city made history and preserved it beautifully in Victorian mansions, cathedrals, 18th-century fortresses and a Queen's Staircase whose 66 steps lead to a view not to be missed. Nassau is home to weatherbeaten, ancient forts; elegant, noble architecture; and fine local and international crafts and goods, from handmade lengths of batik to Rolex watches.

 

Our trip continues with an all inclusive five-day stay at the Sandals Resort in Nassau where hopefully we'll catch some sun, go snorkeling, and do other fun stuff. Can't wait to go and tell you all about it!
