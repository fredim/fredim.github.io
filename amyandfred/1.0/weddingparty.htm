BRIDESMAIDS
 
 

Pearl Tantivongsathaporn
Maid of Honor
Pearl is Amy's "Da Jie" - that's big sister for all you non-Chinese folk. From rescues at the kindergarten playground, to wiping away tears, Pearl has been the sister of strength and courage. As the mastermind behind the Bunkbed Room production of "The Dog That Fell Down the Well", funky dance shows, modeling & photography sessions on the stairway, and the cooking genius of Pearl's Yummi Yum, Pearl taught Amy to take imagination to reality.

 
 

Judy Tantivongsathaporn
Bridesmaid
Judy is Amy's little sister. These two have had their share of teasing, arguing, and hair pulling, fist punching (and a rare occasion of pencil stabbing) moments. Ugly as they may have been, they are comical in retrospect, and are overshadowed in comparison by the abundunce of laughter they shared. Biking adventures, time space traveling (sitting in a tub, making swishing spaceship noises), and Cabbage Patch Kid adventures… without Judy, being a kid would never have been the same.

 
 

Jenny Chu
Bridesmaid
Amy has known Jenny since Junior High School but their friendship didn't take off until high school. Their friendship has only grown stronger through the years as together they have seen each other's worst of times and best of times. High school chemistry will forever be one of the most interesting classes, pigs will always be nasty, refrigerators are never running, "lindberger cheese" will eternally be stinky, and car chases forever banned. More than just best friends, Amy considers Jenny as a sister.
 
 

Junko Sasaki
Bridesmaid
Amy and Junko began their friendship in high school as shopping buddies. Together, they went on a wild adventure through Europe for a month and a half, meeting interesting characters, visiting historic sites, eating…well, sadly enough a lot of McDonald's, and doing what those girls do best: shopping! Their experiences together are memorable to say the least…the laughter, the tears, and bickering too…all make Junko, like Jenny, just another one of Amy's sisters.

 
  Emily Chien
Flowergirl
Emily is Amy's niece, daughter of Amy's cousin Allen. At first, Emily was hesitant to take on the task of flowergirl. However, after reading a short story on the adventures of a flowergirl, this adorable, ultra-girlie five year old is now anxiously awaiting the moment she gets to wear a frilly dress, a tiara, high heel shoes, and of course…the sealer of the deal…lip gloss.

GROOMSMEN
 
 

Trevor Oelschig
Best Man

Fred has known Trevor since high school in Southern California. They first met playing basketball at the Y and their common interests quickly made them best friends. The two were accepted to the University of California, Berkeley where together they joined the same fraternity. Trevor has been an amazing friend and has always been there for Fred.

 
 

Clayton Schupp
Groomsman

Fred first met Clayton in college in their fraternity and lived together for several years in the fraternity and also a few years after college. Because Clayton and Amy were good friends in college, Fred credits Clayton for helping the two get together. Without Clayton, Fred would never have had the chance to get to know Amy. (Thanks Clayton.) Together, Clayton and Fred have traveled parts of Europe together and took on a week long snowboarding adventure in Austria.
 
 

Jason Sanchez
Groomsman

One of Fred's fraternity brothers, Jason ("Rucci") and Fred's common interest over video games, cartoons and snow boarding made them good friends. They've tackled some interesting project together including two Flugtag comptitions in San Francisco (Clayton also participated in both.)

 
 

Ernesto Espinoza
Groomsman

Like Fred's other groomsmen, Ernesto and Fred also met through Acacia fraternity. He has been a wonderful friend who has always been there for Fred. Without Ernesto, college would not have been as intoxicating (haha). After college, Ernesto recruited Fred to work with him in the same company. Together, they also lived together until only a year ago when Fred and Amy bought their condo and Ernesto went with his wife to New York.
