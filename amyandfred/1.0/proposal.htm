SHE SAID - by Amy

 

Every month my best friends Jenny and Junko and I meet for dinner. We take turns choosing a place and give each other scores for the food, ambiance, service and creativity of the restaurant we picked. At the end of the year, the person with the lowest score has to take the other two out for dessert and a movie. This game we play is fun and we constantly try to outdo the other. So when it was Junko's turn, it didn't occur to me that her restaurant selection way out in Half Moon Bay wasn't anything more than a desperate move for the currently losing player to gain some much needed brownie points. The moment we arrived, Jenny pulled out her camera and began snapping pictures of the scene, of me, and of Junko. But if you know Jenny, you know she can be an odd cat at times. Besides, as a twist to our normal restaurant game, we decided that we each had to make a page for a scrapbook that would detail the events of each dinner. I figured she was getting photos for the book. So again, it didn't occur to me that anything was different about this dinner.

The restauarnt we arrived at was the Moss Beach Distillery. We were seated by the panoramic window overlooking the rocky cliff and ocean. I headed towards a seat facing more of the parking lot than the ocean, but as I pulled out my chair Junko stopped me and asked if I'd like to take a better seat to face the ocean. I was already starting to sit down so I thanked her for the offer and took the seat I had already claimed. What nice friends I have, I thought.

It was a quiet night being a Wednesday, so there were only a handful of other patrons. We began looking over the menu and gabbing about normal stuff. But between chats, Junko would frantically play with her cell phone, ranting about how awful her phone was. She must have played with that phone a dozen times, trying to fix something it seemed. For me, it was getting really irritating and between bites I finally told Junko to leave her damn phone alone. After all, we are civilized people and can have a nice dinner without phone calls from boyfriends.

Dinner finished and we began looking over the dessert menu. Then from the corner of my eye, the waitress who had been oddly eying me for the latter part of the night joined a group of other waiters and waitresses as they paraded down the restaurant. I thought, how fun, it's someone's birthday! But who? There were only three other groups here and everyone else seemed to be just as puzzled about the little fiasco. Then I squinted my eyes to get a better look at the tray the leading waitress was holding. It had a little cake, but no candle. Odd, I thought. Where's the birthday candle? Then in the crowd of waiters and waitresses, I swear I see Fred. Not only was it Fred, but it was Fred dressed in his suit. What!? Is that him? What is he doing here? This is MY girls night out. Is it an emergency? Why is he wearing his suit? (All these thoughts happening of course in only seconds.)

Then it hits me. What is that sitting next to the cake on the tray? A red box? A tiny red box? And like that...I suddenly realize as I put together all the moments of the night: The odd restaurant location, the crazy photos, the seating arrangement (they wanted me to face the ocean so I wouldn't see Fred's car pull into the lot), the frantic phone messaging (to warn Fred), the odd waitress, the cake with no candle, Fred, Fred dressed up, and finally the little red box..

And so it happened that night with my two best friends by my side, my knight in shining armour got down on his knees ....

HE SAID - by Fred
 

After talking about it a little before, I spent a couple of months ring shopping. Not like I could tell what the difference was. And after having some input from Amy about what she liked, and disliked, I ordered the ring in April of 2004.

Then, I waited.

Given, that the estimate provided by the jeweller was in the 4-6 week range, the ring certainly was not late by any means. But the waiting just dragged on. There were times, during April and in early May, that I thought, "How convenient it would be if the ring would be finished by this date, so I can propose." But no. It took almost the full 6 weeks.

In fact, it was in the 5th week (still no ring), that this plan was hatched. The girls have a monthly dinner where they take turns picking a place. Since it wasn't Amy's turn, I could pick the place and arrange with her friends to meet them after dinner. So I started calling around, looking for a romantic setting to propose. And I called the jeweller, who was optimistic that the ring would com in on schedule.

In fact, the ring was scheduled to come in the Friday before. But more on that later.

Amy and I both love the coastline, and I thought the most romantic place would be near Half Moon Bay, at sunset. The restaurant rated most romantic down that stretch was in a small cozy building overlooking Moss Beach on a bluff, the Moss Beach Distillery. (They even have a ghost!) Dinner was scheduled with Jenny and Junko to start at 7 so that I could propose around 7:30, around sunset.

I also arranged a room at a bed and breakfast in Half Moon Bay, so we could wander leisurely the next day. Oh, and many thanks to Amy's boss, who I cold-called at work to get her the day off! In fact, I later heard that when I called, he was in a meeting with her.

Back to the waiting! It turns out the ring was not ready on the Friday before. Or the Saturday. The jeweller's weekend being Sunday-Monday, I was quite glad to hear his voice on Saturday, saying that the ring was there! But, alas, he wanted to clean and polish, so I scheduled the pickup on Tuesday. Come Tuesday, as anxious as I was, everything went fairly smoothly. Particularly handing over the second largest check I'd ever written (the first was the downpayment for our
condo).

Finally, the day had come. After work, Jenny and Junko met us over at our place, ready to pick Amy up. On time even! (Good job, Junko!) As they drove off at 6pm, I scrambled. Given an hour, I showered, dressed, packed (for both me and Amy). Crap! It's already 6:30. And then off to the car, drove up and checked in to the B&B. Time check,7. Deep breaths. Don't want to be too early.

Previously, I'd arranged to text-message Junko, so I could let her know I was ready. As I drove up to the secluded beach town location, I noticed a slight flaw in my plan. No signal. So I drove slowly by the restaurant noting the windows, and parked.

One eking signal bar, so I paged Junko, and waited about 5 minutes. I got ansy. It was already 7:30. I called the restaurant. After explaining to the hostess my intentions, she described the layout of the restaurant and how the girls were sitting in a table next to the entryway. So I couldn't go in that way. She told me to come around the side of the restaurant, through the kitchen.

Well, the girls took their sweet time. Probably wondering why I hadn't called, and wondering if they had to stall any longer. Their waitress came out to chat with me, and I ordered dessert for the girls, while I hung out with the chefs. I was so nervous, they offered me a drink.

And then, it was time for dessert. Taking a big gulp, I got in line behind the waitress as we made our way over to the table. Things get blurry from here. I remember seeing Amy with a big smile, and getting on my knee to propose. And she didn't say anything, but she kissed me. The people there cheered for us, so nice of them.

THEY SAID - by Jenny and Junko
 

It all started with Fred IMing us and telling us that he had Amy's ring and was asking for suggestions as to where he should propose. Being the social couple they are, they were booked every single weekend the month of June. At first Fred wanted to fly to Florida and meet up with Amy, who was there for a bachelorette party. Of course that idea was quickly "x'd" out when we calculated the prices and realized Amy would be upset if Fred spent so much money. So Fred asked if he could propose to Amy at our next monthly dinner. Each month the three of us would meet up for dinner and take turns picking out a different ethnic restaurant. We then would grade the restaurant on several criteria and at the end of the year the loser pays for dessert and a movie. Since Junko had not chosen a restaurant, we decided to go with Fred's plan. Fred wanted to pick some place romantic but not too obvious and decided on Moss Beach Distillery. So that Wednesday, we picked Amy up and told her we were heading off to Half Moon Bay for dinner. Amy was clueless since she knew Junko was hurting for points HAHAHAH. Driving up to the restaurant Amy was commenting on how far it was and how windy the road was. "Are we there yet?" "I'm hungry!"
I think we remember Amy commenting on deducting points for the extra long
distance.

When we finally reached the restaurant we tried to get Amy to sit on the side that didn't quite face the parking lot just incase she saw Fred pull up. Luckily, the whole time she was clueless and commenting on how nice the restaurant was. The plan was for Fred to send a text message to us when he arrived but both our phones had really poor reception. During dinner we kept checking our phones to see if either of us had gotten a message from Fred saying he had arrived, which got Amy slightly irritated. (She doesn't like boyfriends calling and disturbing our dinner HAHAHA) Since dinner went by so fast, we decided to order dessert to stall. Then all of a sudden the lights dimmed and the waitress brought out a dessert with Fred behind her looking sharp in his suit. We were both trying to take good pictures but we were too busy crying and giggling at the same time.

As things settled, Fred joined us to eat our dessert and Oh … he paid for our dinner (Amy, he's a keeper!!!)
The rest of the night we pretty much spent admiring them and of course…the
beautiful ring. We said our farewells at the parking lot as they left for their romantic evening alone.

Congrats to our best friend and her husband to be!! Love you guys!
